Synthesizing Images on Perceptual Boundaries of ANNs

Uncovering and Manipulating Human Perceptual Variability with the BAM Framework

BAM: Generating images that evoke high perceptual variability

Project Overview

Human observers show significant individual differences on visual classification tasks. Our work introduces the BAM (Boundary Alignment & Manipulation) framework:

  • Labeling: Sample high-uncertainty images along an ANN’s decision boundary and collect 116,715 human judgments from 246 participants, forming the variMNIST dataset.
  • Aligning: Fine-tune models on human response data to predict both group-level and individual perceptual variability.
  • Manipulating: Use these personalized models to generate “controversial” stimuli that push different observers to make opposing decisions.
